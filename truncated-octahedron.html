Between those three directions sit the three angles — 45° from axis to edge, 35.26° from edge up to corner, 54.74° = arctan√2 from axis straight to corner. They are the Lost Triangle’s own angles, and the axis reference drawn inside the Vector Pod.

Space-filling: this is Kelvin’s cell — the truncated octahedron tiles 3D space by itself. Six neighbours meet it square-to-square along the axes; eight more meet it hexagon-to-hexagon along the corners. The animation packs all 14 around it.

Same parts: Gregg’s built cell shares its 8 corner-hexagon panels, at 1:1 part scale, with the Vector Pod — one kit of parts, two space-filling variations.

The family: cube · rhombic dodecahedron · truncated octahedron — one space-filler per direction family, all generated by 1 : √2 : √3. Gregg’s Vector House lives on that same edge direction: every panel folds at exactly 45°, every level a multiple of √2 — the built form of the middle length.

The lineage: Gregg is picking up directly where Peter Pearce’s Structure in Nature and Robert Williams’ Natural Structure left off — this exact pair, the truncated octahedron and its dual the rhombic dodecahedron, is already named in their space-filling catalogues.
